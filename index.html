Kolkata Visitor Guide

Essential tips for a smooth and enjoyable stay.

General Travel Tips

  • Transportation: Ride-sharing apps like Uber and Ola are reliable and recommended. For a classic experience, use Yellow Taxis but always agree on the fare first.
  • SIM Card: Purchase a local SIM (Airtel, Jio) at the airport with your passport and visa. An eSIM is a convenient alternative if your phone is compatible.
  • Safety: Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night. Keep valuables secure. Kolkata is generally safe for tourists.
  • Hydration: Drink only bottled or filtered water. Avoid tap water.
  • Etiquette: Dress modestly for religious sites (cover shoulders/knees). A "Namaste" greeting is always appreciated.

Must-Try Kolkata Delicacies

Sweets (Mishti)

Don't leave without trying Roshogolla, Mishti Doi (sweet yogurt), and Sandesh.

Street Food

Experience the city's flavors with Phuchka (Kolkata's pani puri), the original Kathi Roll, and spicy Jhalmuri.

Main Courses

Enjoy traditional Macher Jhol (fish curry), Kolkata Biryani (with its signature potato), and Luchi-Alur Dom (fluffy bread with potato curry).

Pre-Trip Checklist (Do This Week)

  • Confirm Flights: Double-check your flight times for any last-minute changes.
  • Passport & Visa: Ensure your passport is valid for at least 6 months and your Indian visa is printed or accessible.
  • Travel Insurance: Purchase or confirm your travel and health insurance policy.
  • Notify Bank: Inform your bank of your travel dates to avoid card issues in India.
  • Currency Exchange: Exchange a small amount of THB for INR for immediate needs upon arrival (e.g., taxi).
  • Download Apps: Install Uber, Ola, and offline Google Maps for Kolkata.
  • Check-in Online: Check-in for your flight 24 hours before departure.

Info for Thai Visitors

  • Currency: Indian Rupee (INR).
  • Exchange Rate: Approx. 1 THB ‚âà 2.30 INR (check live rates before departure).
  • Payments: Credit cards are common in large establishments, but cash is necessary for smaller vendors and transport.
  • Language: English is widely understood in tourist and business settings.
  • Power Sockets: India uses Type C, D, and M. A universal adapter is required. Voltage is 230V.
